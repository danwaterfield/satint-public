Methodology Notes

  • Baseline period: 15 Jan – 27 Feb 2026 (pre-war)
  • % change: (current − baseline) / baseline × 100
  • Compound risk: Weighted composite of 8 normalised factors — NL 20%, Fire 15%, Trade 15%, NO₂ 12%, Internet 12%, Flights 10%, GDELT 8%, Thermal 8%
  • DNB L1B caveat: No lunar illumination or straylight correction — absolute values less reliable than relative day-to-day trends
  • Cloud cover: Tracked per observation as quality indicator, not filtered. High cloud fraction reduces confidence.
  • Ramadan caveat: Baseline is pre-Ramadan. Behavioral changes (later activity, outdoor lighting) may affect nightlight comparisons.
  • Infrastructure “No Data”: Flaring facilities (oil, LNG) normally emit detectable thermal signatures. Absence of FIRMS detections may indicate offline status or a data gap — reported as “No Data” rather than confirmed “Offline”.

Model assumptions & limitations: NZ imports ~40% of refined fuel from refineries dependent on crude transiting the Strait of Hormuz (configurable parameter). Depletion model assumes linear consumption at current rates and constant Hormuz disruption fraction. Stock data from MBIE quarterly reports may be up to 90 days stale — projected onshore stock adjusts for elapsed days. Cape of Good Hope rerouting (+14 days transit) applied when Hormuz disruption exceeds 30%. Industry cascade thresholds are domain estimates from NZ energy policy literature, not empirically calibrated. Sigmoid scoring prevents any single extreme input from saturating the composite — a total Hormuz closure (100% disruption) scores 0.92, not 1.0.
