Key Findings

Mar 10 2026
Mar 6–7: Synchronized Gulf-Wide Infrastructure Collapse

By cross-correlating raw VIIRS DNB nightlight swaths with FIRMS thermal anomaly data, a two-stage compound event is visible across the entire Gulf region simultaneously:

MAR 6
Strike day. High fire-radiative-power anomalies at Ras Laffan LNG complex, Qatar — 71 MW at 2.6 km, 57 MW at 2.1 km. Peak fire activity: Iran 35 fires >30 MW, Saudi Arabia 42, Iraq 33. Nightlights begin dropping: Dubai −26%, Riyadh −46%.
MAR 7
Grid collapse signal. Industrial fire activity falls ~80% simultaneously across all countries (Iran 35→7, Saudi 42→10, Iraq 33→2). When a power grid fails, industrial flaring and combustion stop together. Nightlights: Dubai −60%, Riyadh −67%, Abu Dhabi −41%, Kuwait City −38%, Basra −55%. Not cloud contamination — clear-sky DNB retrievals confirmed.
MAR 8
Tehran approaching blackout. Most Gulf cities partially recover. Tehran reaches −78% vs baseline — a city of 16M people at 22% normal illumination. Persistent 82–103 MW fire cluster at 30.75°N 48.28°E (Khuzestan/Iraq border, 2+ days) consistent with sustained oil infrastructure burning.
ANOMALY
Doha (Qatar) maintains positive readings throughout (+7% to +32%). Qatar hosts Al Udeid Air Base (largest US base in the Middle East). Consistently protected while the rest of the Gulf went dark on Mar 6–7.

Caveats: DNB L1B has no lunar or straylight correction — absolute % values are less reliable than day-to-day trend changes within the same city. Riyadh's persistent −40–47% may partly reflect Ramadan behavioral changes (baseline is pre-Ramadan January). Sources: NASA FIRMS, NASA LAADS DAAC (VNP02DNB/VNP03DNB), Sentinel-1 via ASF DAAC.
